My Favourite Cities

As human beings, we are naturally drawn to different places for various reasons, whether it is for our culture, architecture, history, or beauty. Some cities leave a lasting impression on us and become our favorites, a place we yearn to return to and reminisce about. I will share my personal experiences and insights about my favorite cities, each of which holds a special place in my heart. From the bustling & busy streets of Lahore to the serene landscapes of Islamabad, these cities have enriched my life in many ways and continue to inspire me.

Islamabad: City Of Trees

Islamabad is the capital city of Pakistan and was founded in 1960.The city is known for its stunning architecture, particularly its modernist buildings, such as the Faisal Mosque and the Pakistan Monument.Islamabad is also home to numerous parks and green spaces, including the Margalla Hills National Park, which is a popular destination for hiking and outdoor activities.Islamabad is a major hub for diplomacy and international relations, with many embassies and diplomatic missions located in the city.

Lahore: The Heart Of Pakistan

Lahore is a historic city located in the northeast of Pakistan, known for its rich culture, beautiful architecture, and delicious food. It is often referred to as the cultural capital of Pakistan and is home to many historic landmarks, such as the Lahore Fort and the Badshahi Mosque. With its vibrant street life and bustling bazaars, Lahore offers a unique and unforgettable experience to visitors from all over the world. The famous saying about Lahore is "Jine Lahore nai takkeya, o jamya nai" which translates to "One who has not seen Lahore, has not been born." This saying reflects the city's rich history, vibrant culture, and the deep attachment that people feel towards Lahore.
